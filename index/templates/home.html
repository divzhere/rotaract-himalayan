Adult Literacy

in the slum areas of chandigarh

This tenure Rotaract Himalayan aims at educating 300+ of all those young adults older than 15 years living in slum areas; who due to their fate and poverty are not able to get the even basic education. The task of a modern educator is not to cut down the jungles but to irrigate deserts. The Director of this project, Rotaractor Sapna Devi believes that education is not preparation for life but life itself and has aspired to make these adults educated so at least it is they become self-sufficient to read and write and avoid hindrance in their lives. The Club has collaborated with Infosys Chandigarh to provide for funds and to organise workshops for the Rotaractors itself to learn the basic teaching skills needed. The surveys have been conducted and are in continuation in the slum areas of Chandigarh which are majorly located in sector 25 and 38 west. Moreover, the villages of Chandigarh like Maloya, and Dhanas are too on the survey and education list.

  • Director: Sapna Devi

Back to School

educating school dropouts

Even if Right to Education was made a Fundamental Right in 2009, but there are still hundreds of children who can't get this right and thus Himalayan family has come in their way to help them. Just like Project Adult Literacy, this project too aims at educating but in this case the children who are school dropouts and render them certified education. The Director of this project, Rotaractor Aakriti goes with the simple and golden thought of changing peoples' life is a no less than a great charity. The Club has collaborated with local organisations Jan Shiksha Sansthan and Arpan for various activities required in this initiative.

  • Director: Aakriti

Animal Welfare

raising awareness about animals

Project Animal Welfare has started in the pursuit of making people cognizant about these beings around them who need a voice to express their pain. Rotaractor Ananta Kant is the Director of this project and believes that not all valiant wear a white cape, some have flying ears. This project is dedicated to make society aware about the adoption of street dogs and not just royal dog breeds. The project also aims to educate people through animal welfare campaigns and demonstrations that focuses on to highlight how humans are making the lives miserable of animals. The project has two main events i.e. Bodhi and Vegan Food Fest which pay attention to importance of adopting vegan food in our lives which is beneficial both for us Humans and animals.

  • Director: Ananta Kant
  • Events: Bodhi, Vegan Food Fest

Skill Development

providing vocational training

It is possible to fly without motors but not without knowledge and skill. For those who are determined to fly, having no wings in just a detail. Rotaractor Pulkit Chanana, the Director of this project came up with a vision to bridge the colossal aperture between skills and their judicious use. The project Skill Development caters to provide vocational training in various fields. Post extensive cogitation and tenacious endeavours, Rotaract Himalayan has been successful in collaborating with Desh Bhagat University to try to instill skills in the youth so that they can contribute to improve India’s jobless growth story while also promoting entrepreneurial spirit. Earmarking all efforts to achieve the target of 50 certified youth to get meaningful jobs by the end of the tenure, the Skill Development wants to ensure basic sustainability to the underprivileged.

  • Director: Pulkit Chanana

Happy School

transforming schools

One of the successful projects of the club has been Project Happy School. It is an initiative under the TEACH program of Rotary India Literacy Mission. Every year the club adopts a school which lacks basic schooling facilities like benches, sanitation, uniforms, books, proper electricity and security etc. We put our hearts out in making each school a little happier place to be at, to give these kids an environment they deserve and cherish. The proceeds raised through our various fundraisers like Pirates of the City and Salsa Slam are allocated to build the infrastructure of the Happy School. In the last tenure, four schools were adopted and transformed completely. This tenure around five more schools are added to the list and the work has already proceeded. Proper education is a basic human right. It breaks the vicious circle of poverty, hunger, unemployment and so many social evils that still exists in.
